# System Rules

Twelve non-negotiable operating constraints. When violated, the delivery system degrades. Enforcement is explicit by design.

Source: The Nolte Way — Delivery Playbook.

## 1. Single Decision-Maker Rule

Each client engagement must name one accountable business decision-maker. This person owns priorities, tradeoffs, and go/no-go decisions. Stakeholders may advise; they do not decide. Missed decisions delay new commitments, not delivery already in progress.

## 2. Commitment Is a One-Way Door

Once work crosses the Commitment Point (Ready → In Implementation), capacity is allocated, tradeoffs are locked, delivery is expected. Changes after commitment require explicit de-scoping or reprioritization and acknowledgment of impact on other committed work.

## 3. No Scope Evolution During Validation

Validation confirms agreed behavior. It does not redefine it. New ideas discovered in validation return to Backlog. Story Defects must map to agreed examples. Validation failures pause acceptance; they do not expand scope.

## 4. WIP Limits Are Enforced, Not Advisory

Work-in-progress limits are system constraints. No new work enters In Specification or In Implementation when limits are exceeded. Exceptions require Head of Product approval and are logged. Chronic violations trigger a system review.

**Exception logging format (machine-readable):** Exceptions are logged as a comment by an authorized approver during the In Implementation window, containing the exact phrase `WIP exception:` followed by the justification. The grader (dimension D7) scans for this phrase when checking WIP compliance. Comments without this exact prefix are not recognized as logged exceptions.

## 5. Outcome Drift Triggers Re-Alignment

If delivered work fails to impact the intended business objective, new work is paused, epic objectives are re-evaluated, sequencing is adjusted before further commitment. Delivery does not continue on autopilot.

## 6. Priority Changes Require Explicit Tradeoffs

Any new priority must name what is delayed or removed. Sneaking work in is not permitted. Urgent work is reclassified through Classes of Service.

## 7. Decision Latency Is a System Risk

When required decisions are not made within the weekly cadence, the system defaults to existing priorities, new commitments pause, escalation occurs by exception.

## 8. Metrics Ownership Is Explicit

Nolte aligns delivery to business metrics. The client owns instrumentation, reporting, and interpretation. Nolte flags missing or weak signals but does not invent data.

## 9. Releases Are Reporting Units, Not Deadlines

Monthly releases exist to review delivered work, reflect on outcomes, align stakeholders. They are not delivery commitments or sprint deadlines. Timeboxes do not create predictability — flow does.

## 10. Internal Work Is Never Hidden

Internal initiatives are explicitly classified as Internal, have separate WIP limits, do not consume client capacity invisibly.

## 11. Repeated Ambiguity Triggers Pause

If work repeatedly fails specification or validation due to unclear objectives, delivery pauses, alignment is re-established. Work does not continue "to keep things moving."

## 12. Pause and Exit Conditions

Delivery may pause or disengage if: priorities change faster than weekly alignment; a decision-maker is unavailable for multiple cycles; validation consistently fails due to misalignment; delivery no longer ties to a meaningful business outcome.

## System Promise

If priorities are clear and decisions are made on time, Nolte will deliver predictably, surface risk early, tie work to business outcomes, and prevent small issues from becoming expensive failures.

This is the operational contract behind The Nolte Way.
