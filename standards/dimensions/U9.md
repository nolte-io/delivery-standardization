# U9 — Story defects classified

**Lane:** Upstream · **Owner:** Yanna · **Evidence type:** Deterministic + Judge

## What it checks

Each Story Defect raised during validation is classified (functional, design, UX, regulatory, performance). The judge additionally determines whether the defect maps to an agreed scenario in the parent Story, or represents scope evolution.

## Why it matters

Not every defect caught during validation is a quality failure. Some are scope evolution — things that weren't in the original contract. Distinguishing the two is a specification-quality signal: if scope-evolution defects dominate, the specs are systematically under-specifying. The grader does not deny quality fixes; it measures where the real gap is.

## How the grader evaluates it

- **Deterministic:** Story Defect has a classification tag.
- **Judge:** Claude reads the defect description and the parent Story's scenarios at commit time, and categorizes as `DEFECT_MAPS_TO_SCENARIO` or `DEFECT_IS_SCOPE_EVOLUTION`.
