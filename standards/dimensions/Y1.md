# Y1 — Business objective nameable

**Lane:** Upstream · **Owner:** Yanna · **Evidence type:** Deterministic + Judge

## What it checks

Every Story must link to an Epic, and the Epic must state its business objective in business terms — revenue, retention, activation, risk reduction, cost savings, time savings, compliance. Not a technical task. Not "improve UX."

## Why it matters

If no one can name the business outcome a delivery serves, the work cannot be validated, cannot be prioritized against alternatives, and cannot be explained to a client. It also cannot be killed when it stops mattering — which is the posture Nolte is built on.

## How the grader evaluates it

Two checks:

1. **Deterministic:** Story has an Epic Link. The Epic's description contains a `## Business Objective` section with content.
2. **Judge:** Claude reads the objective text and determines whether it names a concrete business outcome in business vocabulary, not a technical deliverable in disguise.

Judge prompt: [`../../prompts/Y1.md`](../../prompts/Y1.md).

## Evidence codes

| Code | Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `BUSINESS_OBJECTIVE_IN_BUSINESS_TERMS` | PASS | Objective names a concrete, business-measurable outcome. |
| `OBJECTIVE_IS_TECHNICAL_NOT_BUSINESS` | FAIL | States only a feature, capability, or technical task. |
| `OBJECTIVE_TOO_VAGUE_TO_VERIFY` | FAIL | No observable outcome could verify success. |
| `OBJECTIVE_TEXT_MISSING` | FAIL | Section missing or empty. |
| `EPIC_LINK_MISSING` | FAIL | Story has no Epic Link. |

See [`../evidence-codes.md`](../evidence-codes.md) for the full catalog.
