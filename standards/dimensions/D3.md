# D3 — Violations surfaced, not absorbed

**Lane:** Downstream · **Owner:** Hector · **Evidence type:** Judge

## What it checks

When execution deviates from the agreed scenarios, it is flagged explicitly in comments or a sub-task — not silently absorbed into the delivered work.

## Why it matters

Silent absorption is the most corrosive failure mode in a delivery system. It delivers something the team didn't agree to, hides the deviation from the approver, and erodes the forecast. The right response when implementation can't execute the agreed behavior is to stop and surface — not to quietly ship something different.

This is the noisiest dimension by design — detecting a negative is genuinely hard. Candidate for Opus upgrade via `judge.model_by_dimension` if Sonnet proves unreliable.

## How the grader evaluates it

Judge. Claude reads comments and sub-task descriptions during the In Implementation window against the agreed scenarios. Detects linguistic signals of deviation without a corresponding flag.

Judge prompt: [`../../prompts/D3.md`](../../prompts/D3.md).
