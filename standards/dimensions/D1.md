# D1 — Sub-tasks created after commit

**Lane:** Downstream · **Owner:** Hector · **Evidence type:** Deterministic

## What it checks

Sub-task creation timestamps are at or after the Story's commitment timestamp (Done Specifying → In Implementation).

## Why it matters

Sub-tasks created before commitment are planning artifacts — they treat specification as a design exercise rather than an alignment exercise. The playbook says sub-tasks support execution, not planning. Pre-commit sub-tasks signal that engineering is being asked to pre-decompose work that isn't yet ready to be decomposed.

## How the grader evaluates it

Deterministic. Compares each sub-task's `created` timestamp against the commitment transition timestamp. Any sub-task created before commit = fail for the Story.

## Evidence codes

| Code | Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `SUBTASKS_CREATED_POST_COMMIT` | PASS | All sub-tasks created after commitment. |
| `SUBTASKS_CREATED_PRE_COMMIT` | FAIL | One or more created before. |
| `SUBTASKS_ABSENT_ON_NON_TRIVIAL` | FAIL | Complex Story has no sub-tasks. |

## Current rollup signal

- Fail rate: **98%** (57 / 58 graded Stories).
- Nearly every Story has sub-tasks created before commitment. Either sub-task creation is being used as the specification artifact, or there's a template-based auto-generation pattern. Discipline failure — but worth confirming whether it's behavior or tooling.

## Open issues

- D1 triage — engineering discipline review: when are sub-tasks being created, by whom, and why pre-commit? Fix the behavior or fix the tool that's auto-creating them.
